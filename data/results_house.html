    Analysts expected control of the House to flip to Democrats after this years midterm elections, when all 435 seats were up for grabs. About two dozen Republican-held districts that backed Democrat Hillary Clinton in 2016 were among the most vulnerable for the GOP.

    Which House seats were vulnerable

    Democrats needed a net gain of 23 seats to win control of the House. Here's the result for each seat and how likely it was to flip, based on pre-race ratings from Cook Political Report. So far Democrats have gained 38 seats.
